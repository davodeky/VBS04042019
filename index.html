Sunday's Sermon

Bible Reading

Theme: Arise and Shine
  • Philippians 4:10-13
  • 1 Corinthians 15:57

Sunday's Sermon
  • Paul is reminding us that no matter the circumstance, we can do all things through Christ who is the source of our strength.
  • With the level of technology and knowledge present now, it is easy to be tempted to think that we do not need God now.
  • When we only trust the knowledge that we have, we get limited by only what we can conceive.
  • Our trust in the strength of God in us gives us the ability to go beyond these limitations.
  • Things in life have a tendency of letting us lose sight of the value that we have before God, the love that He has for us.
  • Paul is reminding us that our resumes are good enough.
  • What we can do does not depend on what we can see or touch, but on the permanence of the strength of He who works through us.
  • God can use trials to increase our capacity.
  • Trials are not a punishment, but a test of strength and a test of our faith.
  • Sometimes, trials are the way to a promotion.
  • There is testimony without a test.
  • God also shows up in our lives through victories.

Disciplines For The Disciple

7. The Discipline of Teaching

Introduction
We have thus far looked at the disciplines of:

  • prayer- secret, simple, and steadfast.
  • Meditation- contemplating God, His works, His words, and things worthy of virtue.
  • fasting- humbling oneself before God when joined with sincere prayer.
  • singing- which edifies the singer as well as praises God.
  • Fellowship- communing with other Christians as we engage in spiritual activities.
  • Giving- sharing sacrificially of money, time, and ability as God's grace allows.

If we practice the disciplines listed above,we will:
  1. Undoubtedly grow in grace and knowledge as disciples of Christ.
  2. Likely be able to share the benefits of such growth with others.
This naturally leads us to the discipline of teaching;
  1. Another spiritual exercise that can help us become more like Christ.
  2. Jesus Christ taught in public and in private.

Questions
  1. Are we all supposed to be teachers?
  2. What are the benefits of teaching?
  3. What does the bible teach us about the practice of teaching?
